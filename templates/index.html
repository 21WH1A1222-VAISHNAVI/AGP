What is AGP?


An ambulatory glucose profile (AGP) is a comprehensive report that provides a visual representation of a person's glucose levels over a period of time,typically spanning 24 hours or several days. It's generated from continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) data, which tracks glucose levels in real-time throughout the day and night.
The AGP displays various metrics such as average glucose, standard deviation, time in range, time above range (hyperglycemia), and time below range (hypoglycemia). AGP offers a holistic view of glucose patterns, trends, and variability, allowing healthcare providers and individuals with diabetes to assess glycemic control, identify potential issues, and adjust treatment plans accordingly.
AGPs are valuable tools in diabetes management as they provide deeper insights into glucose dynamics compared to traditional blood glucose monitoring methods, enabling more personalized and effective diabetes care.
